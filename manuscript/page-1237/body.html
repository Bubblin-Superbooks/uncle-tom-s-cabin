One of the other men struck up one of those unmeaning songs, common among the slaves.

“Mas’r see’d me cotch a coon, High boys, high! He laughed to split,—d’ye see the moon, Ho! ho! ho! boys, ho! Ho! yo! hi—e! oh!”

The singer appeared to make up the song to his own pleasure, generally hitting on rhyme, without much attempt at reason; and the party took up the chorus, at intervals,

“Ho! ho! ho! boys, ho! High—e—oh! high—e—oh!”

It was sung very boisterouly, and with a forced attempt at merriment; but no wail of despair, no words of impassioned prayer, could have had such a depth of woe in them as the wild notes of the chorus.
